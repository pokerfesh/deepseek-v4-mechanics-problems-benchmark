Abstract: The study measures the text-based problem solving accuracy of DeepSeek V4 Pro and Flash in mechanics. The research aims to solve three questions: (1) both model’s overall accuracy across the full sample, (2) accuracy by chapter, (3) and the magnitude and statistical genuineness of accuracy gap between models. The models were evaluated using a set of 416 odd-numbered end chapter problems under the 14-chapter mechanics unit of 15th Edition University Physics Textbook by Young & Freedman. Each problem, along with a system prompt and temperature parameter set to 0, were sent as a single API call. The correctness of both model’s answers were evaluated manually against the available answer key. Wilson’s score interval (95% CI) was applied as another statistical layer to assess the genuine performance gap between models by accounting the small and uneven problem spread by chapter. Overall, DeepSeek V4 Pro achieved 95.43% accuracy while DeepSeek V4 Flash achieved 94.71% accuracy. Both models scored identically in 10 out of 14 chapters. The 4 non-identical scoring chapters attained single-digit percentage gaps ranging from 3.33 to 5.88%. Among those 4 chapters, Pro outperformed Flash in 3 chapters, while Flash only outscored the other beating in 1 chapter. Overlap factors between models for both overall each 14 chapters exceed the 0.5 mark, indicating that the observed accuracy gaps are merely descriptive and do not suggest any genuine performance gap
